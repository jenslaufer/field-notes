# John Krakauer on the brain, learning & the mind

> One long argument in two moves. **Move 1 (the brain):** stop hunting for the
> circuit that explains everything — there is no privileged foundational level,
> and the discipline that matters is starting from *behavior*, decomposing the
> task, and only *then* going to the neurons — for confirmation, not discovery.
> **Move 2 (the mind):** most intelligence is "smart dumb algorithms" that need
> no understanding at all; but the human capacity for meaning, symbols and
> theorizing is a genuine **discontinuity** — a new thing evolution stumbled onto,
> not a smooth extrapolation from a curious cat. Both AI and neuroscience are
> stuck on the *same* gap, and the honest move is to not eviscerate the mystery
> prematurely.

John is the neurologist brother of SFI president **David Krakauer** (whose
[`landscape-of-21st-century-science.md`](landscape-of-21st-century-science.md) and
[`complexity-science.md`](complexity-science.md) sit next to this note). He also
opened Melanie Mitchell's [`nature-of-intelligence.md`](nature-of-intelligence.md)
season — the *intelligence ≠ competence* distinction there is his.

## Part 1 — Taking multiple perspectives on the brain

- **Neuroscience needs behavior (the viral 2017 paper).** The reductionist bias:
  people run a throwaway behavior just "to get the neurons going," then dive into
  the brain — 49 neural figures, 1 behavior figure — and tell a story after the
  fact. Krakauer inverts the direction of discovery. Use **Marr's levels**
  (computational / algorithmic / implementational): first do the *task analysis*
  at the behavioral level, decompose it into algorithms, compare models, posit a
  variable (a reward- or sensory-prediction error). *Then* go into the brain — to
  **break the tie / confirm**, not to discover. "You go into the brain almost for
  confirmation, not for discovery."
- **A neural correlate is not a mechanism.** Neural data and behavioral data are
  just *two sources of evidence*; neither is automatically more fundamental. Finding
  a correlate doesn't hand you the mechanism.
- **"Filler terms" — the tell of a hidden non-explanation.** When a correlation
  feels unexplanatory, people restate it with a word that sounds deeper: this
  activity *underlies* / *is the basis for* / *represents* the behavior. Those words
  do zero extra conceptual work. They just re-dress the correlation.
- **Understanding ≠ fixing.** Two different projects that get conflated.
- **No privileged level, no "one ring to rule them all"** (David Bohm). SFI is
  *not* seeking the one theory. Pluralism is a **deep metaphysical / ontological
  truth**, not just a human filing limitation: the nervous system itself builds
  *different control objects* as you go up the neuro-axis — which is *why* we have
  both neurology and psychiatry, both psychology and neuroscience. The disciplines
  are picking up real ontological seams, not arbitrary carve-ups.
- **Causal contrast (Carl Craver's tool).** "Why did Socrates die?" — the Athenian
  authorities condemned him / he chose hemlock over exile / the poison hit these
  receptors → respiratory arrest. **All equally true.** You must *pick* your causal
  contrast. The sin is choosing one level's question and answering it with another
  level's materials — or the **hubris of jumping vertical levels**: "you want to
  believe your particular horizontal level of work is the ring that rules them all."
- **The current juncture in neuroscience.** The old Sherringtonian "circuit"
  picture (neuron pings neuron like billiard balls; you *see* the stretch reflex
  and go "ah, I get it") gets a 2.0 from optogenetics/transgenics — but it stalls
  at higher cognition. The rival is *population-level* emergent "**neurodynamic
  objects**" (do math on thousands of neurons at once). Open question: will those
  new objects *replace* psychological constructs, or only ever *confirm* them?
  You can draw the circuit for a knee-jerk or an eye movement; there is **no
  diagram to draw** for why a patient can't retrieve the word "thumb." Different
  phenomena demand different *flavors* of explanatory object.
- **The Learning Salon** (his side project, with Ida Momennejad). Invert the
  conference: 10–15 min of talk, **two hours of questions**. Flatten the prestige
  hierarchy (an undergrad from India gets the same floor as a senior prof), engineer
  radical diversity of discipline and person, revive the **second-person "knowledge
  by presence."** Around hour two "something collective begins to happen… a band of
  slightly drunken friends late at night trying to hash things out" — which he
  argues is *how science actually progresses*. Part of a countercultural stack
  (bioRxiv, eLife, *Brain Inspired*, science Twitter) leveling against the journal
  / tenure / prestige machine.

## Part 2 — Learning, curiosity & consciousness

- **Learning is prospective, not retrospective.** The "Prospective Learning: Back
  to the Future" paper (Future Learning Collective — Josh Vogelstein, Konrad
  Kording; Krakauer was the in-house **critic**). Today's AI *interpolates, doesn't
  extrapolate*: brittle, breaks out-of-distribution, suffers **catastrophic
  interference** (learn task 2, clobber task 1). Animals don't — they're flexible,
  lifelong, multi-task. The project: marry ML's sophisticated *mathematics of
  learning* to neuroscience's *impoverished* treatment of it. "An animal lab in
  the basement of DeepMind."
- **Anticipation ≠ prediction.** Anticipation is cheap, hardwired-plus-learned
  reflex machinery. Prediction is stronger — it implies a **model** (a *substitute
  for the world* you can operate on and simulate). Krakauer's heresy: true
  simulation rarely happens; mostly it's anticipation + heuristics. You navigate
  home, find your hotel room, forage — almost never by conjuring a full map and
  simulating over it.
- **The core fight: degree vs kind.** Vogelstein draws *continuity* from animal to
  human cognition; Krakauer argues **discontinuity**. Human meaning / symbols /
  semantics / language is "a different universe we stumbled across" — a new weapon,
  not an embellishment on the cat's exploratory circling.
- **Curiosity one vs curiosity two.** A cat circling a new box (an evolved
  explore/exploit setting — even primates keep sampling the low-probability option)
  is *not the same thing* as wanting to know what's inside a black hole, or how
  *Game of Thrones* ends. They look alike only at a coarse granularity ("both
  goal-oriented"). "You can have **substrate continuity and functional
  discontinuity**" — my arm and a bird's wing are both skin-bone-muscle; my arm is
  not a little bit of a wing.
- **"Smart dumb algorithms" do 99% of the lifting.** Anticipatory postural
  adjustments (grab a handle, get yanked, don't fall — and the response silently
  accounts for the coffee cup in your other hand); the first muscle to fire when
  you press an elevator button is your calf. Massively intelligent, zero
  understanding, zero awareness. You can be intelligent in many ways *without ever
  needing meaning*.
- **The universe happens to be comprehensible — and evolution stumbled on tissue
  that can comprehend it.** An *accident*, but world-domination-useful. The brain
  was "ready with its capacity to understand calculus as soon as Newton invented
  it," yet a chimp raised in the same culture never gets there → it's a **capacity**,
  not just cultural embeddedness (he flatly rejects the *Cognitive Gadgets* /
  "rodent + culture = cognition" story as a non-starter).
- **The honest epistemic stance.** We have *no form of explanation* for this
  capacity yet — and the absence of AGI plus the unexplained chimp-human gap are
  "**evidence of the same conceptual gap in both fields.**" Resist the urge to
  "eviscerate the mystery" by cutting it down to information theory / deep learning
  / LLMs prematurely. It may turn out to be a fiction (the Dennett/Blackmore
  direction — which he rejects) or a **real problem awaiting a revolutionary
  framework** (his bet) — "but I'm not gonna be prematurely anesthetized out of
  worrying about it." There is no philosophy of the liver; something about *this*
  organ ties us in a knot, and we don't yet know why.

## Für dich — solo-builder read (running an LLM business on agents)

- **Krakauer's inversion IS your agent discipline.** "Get the neurons going, then
  tell a story" = pointing an agent at a vague behavior and admiring the output.
  The correct order is his: **decompose the task first** (the spec / QG scaffold),
  build the model, and treat the mechanism (the LLM, its internals) as
  *confirmation*, not the source of the design. This is exactly why *verbatim spec
  + 1 PR/repo/night + QG* beats "trust a smarter model" — the scaffolding is the
  algorithmic-level work; the model is just the implementational level.
- **A neural correlate ≠ a mechanism → a right answer ≠ understanding.** An agent
  emitting the correct diff or the correct summary is a *correlate*. Don't upgrade
  it to "the agent understood the ticket." And watch your own **filler terms**:
  when you write that an agent "reasons about" or "grasps" the task, check whether
  that word is doing conceptual work or just re-dressing "the output looked right."
- **Name understanding-vs-fixing on every job.** Jens mostly buys *fixing* (a
  working automation for a bottleneck). Don't pay for understanding when a fix is
  the deliverable — and don't *claim* understanding when you only fixed. This is
  the honest frame for the whole KI-Sprint offer, too.
- **Pick your causal contrast before answering.** Your recurring trap (the "3★ vs
  keywords" mix-up Jens flagged) is a *causal-contrast error* in Krakauer's exact
  sense: answering a question posed at one level with materials from another. When
  a PR fails or a channel looks dead, state the causal contrast out loud first,
  then reach for the matching evidence.
- **Degree-vs-kind is a discipline against anthropomorphizing the agent.** The
  meaning-making that *looks* like human cognition may be a different algorithmic
  universe that only rhymes at coarse granularity. Practical consequence: **measure
  at the output ("am Eingang"), never at inferred understanding** — the same rule
  you already apply to distribution.
- **The Learning Salon is a serendipity engine (Bahn B).** Invert the ratio (less
  broadcast, more real dialogue), flatten the hierarchy, engineer diversity, and
  let the collective thing emerge — cheap, high-variance, measured *at the entrance*
  (did the conversation happen?), never at revenue. Its one hard requirement is
  *other people*, which is precisely the gap in your no-audience position — so it's
  a Bahn-B move, not a Bahn-A one.
