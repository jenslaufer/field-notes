# 23 AI Trends Keeping Me Up at Night

> Greg's solo brain-dump of the AI shifts he can't stop thinking about: the
> **one-hour company stack**, **ambient/autonomous businesses**, the **agent
> economy** (Gartner: ~20% of commerce agent-to-agent by 2030; agent market to
> **$52B by 2030**), and **vertical AI** (YC: 300+ vertical-AI unicorns this
> decade). Core thesis: **build cost is ~zero, niches are wide open, audiences
> are underpriced** — and that window is ~12-24 months.

## The opportunity trends

- **One-hour company stack** — grab a validated idea (ideabrowser.com), vibe-code
  it, ship a landing page, wire Stripe, get a first customer — all in a morning.
  New timeline: idea 9:00am → built 9:15 → product 9:45 → first customer 10:00 →
  iterate by lunch. *Monetize:* run a portfolio/machine of experiments, not one bet.
- **Ambient businesses** — companies that run on **zero/low daily human input**;
  agents monitor markets, find opportunities, execute, handle support. You check
  in every few days. Greg expects ambient businesses to hit **7-8 figures**.
- **The agent economy (2025-2030)** — apps era (2009-15) → API era (2015-24) →
  agents discovering/hiring agents now. *Idea:* the **"Glassdoor of AI agents"** —
  reputation/marketplace for which agents to hire (cf. the agent social network
  reportedly acquired by Meta for ~$200M).
- **Agents hiring agents / org-chart of agents** — CEO agent, sales agent, dev
  agents spinning up subtasks and shutting them down (he name-checks the
  open-source **Paperclip**). Reframe: hire-and-manage agents, don't just prompt.
- **Vertical AI > vertical SaaS** — SaaS captures IT budget ($10-100M outcomes);
  **vertical AI taps the labor P&L — replaces headcount — a ~10x bigger TAM.**
  Sell outcomes, not seats. Pick a **sub-niche wedge** of a big category
  (insurance, legal, logistics, elder care) where the YCs aren't fighting yet.
- **Boring gold-mine verticals** — industries on phone calls and faxes: insurance
  (30-yr actuary tables), legal, logistics, elder care, govt accounting,
  construction. Go niche, avoid heavy red-tape categories (selling to govt is hard).
- **Outcome-based pricing** — seats → usage → **pay-per-result** ($1.50/resolved
  ticket vs. $100/seat). Gartner: 40% of enterprise SaaS shifts to outcome-based
  by 2030; 83% of AI-native SaaS already has. *Idea:* a business that converts
  legacy SaaS to outcome pricing — or just build outcome-native startups yourself.
- **The SaaS graveyard** — likely to die: generic CRMs, basic analytics
  dashboards, template marketplaces, scheduling tools, basic chatbot support.
  Survives: vertical workflow tools that pivot to agents, infra, and data moats.
- **Scarcity flip / judgment over execution** — code, generic content, basic
  design, data entry get commoditized; **creative judgment, human-made craft,
  weirdness, proprietary data** become premium.
- **The "human-made" premium** — like an *organic* label for food, expect a
  "no-AI" certification tier (cf. **Porsche's 100% human-made ad campaign**).
  Tiers: human-made (most premium) > AI-assisted/human-led > fully-AI (commodity).
- **IRL / experience economy** — when digital is infinite, scarcity moves to
  physical presence: karaoke, escape rooms, immersive theatre, co-working, live music.
- **Founder-agent fit** — the new founder-market fit: can you **orchestrate a
  fleet of agents** toward a goal? Think like a **film director** — you don't hold
  the camera, you get performances out of your (now machine) actors.
- **Ghost team org chart** — future "team" pages = 2 humans + named, personality'd
  agents (sales, content, support) that will soon voice/video-chat back.
- **The 100 true fans** — agents cut costs so hard that **100 customers at
  $50-$1,000/mo is a real business.** Micro-monopoly math: 5,000-person niche
  audience → app in 48h → 100 customers × $50/mo, run by agents → **~$60K profit,
  one person** — then incubate more.
- **Holding companies of AI-native businesses** — own many agent-run companies in
  adjacent niches, each with a ghost team.

## The things that scare him (also = startup ideas)

- **Agent attack surface** — prompt injection, poisoned context windows, malicious
  MCP servers, agent-to-agent manipulation, permission escalation. Palo Alto
  Networks already documents real-world agent-injection attacks. *"Context-window
  poisoning is the new phishing"* — and potentially far more dangerous because
  agents have system access. Huge cybersecurity-startup opening.
- **Agent permission stack / digital hygiene** — what can your agent access
  (files, email, calendars, bank accounts), remember, do, and share? *Idea/habit:*
  **quarterly agent permission cleanses**, like reviewing app OAuth access.

## Marketing tips

- **Build distribution with AI, before you need it.** The one-hour stack only
  closes if you already have an email list / niche audience to sell the first 100
  customers to. Distribution is the real bottleneck, not the build.
- **Micro-monopoly funnel:** a 100-5,000-person engaged niche audience is enough.
  Don't have one? **Buy it** — build a machine that ships high-quality Meta ads;
  it eats margin but margins are 95% on agent-run products anyway.
- **Build in public — contrarian take.** Conventional wisdom flipped to "don't
  build in public (you invite copycats)." Greg argues benefits win when **your
  followers are your customers**: they vote on the roadmap, become co-builders,
  trust compounds, and you ship updates in 1-5 days. Treat **forking a business**
  (like forking a repo) as inevitable — community + speed is the moat.
- **First-mover on outcome pricing is a sales hook** — leading a cold email or
  newsletter with "pay only per result" is itself a differentiator that lets you
  charge more.

## Why this matters for us

- **One-hour company stack = the launch-kit thesis literally.** Idea validation →
  vibe-code → landing page → Stripe → first customer in a morning. The bottleneck
  is the audience/email list, which is exactly the funnel layer to own.
- **Outcome-based pricing** is a copyable positioning move for any tool we ship —
  "pay per result" as the headline offer, not per seat.
- **Micro-monopoly math (100 × $50/mo, agent-run, ~$60K profit/1 person)** is a
  clean unit-economics target for a small marketable feature.
- **Build-in-public + community-as-co-builders** is a distribution-first growth
  loop we can run on our own audience.
