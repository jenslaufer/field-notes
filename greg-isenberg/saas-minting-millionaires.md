# SaaS is minting millionaires again (the playbook)

> The modern solo-SaaS thesis: AI makes building near-free, so the winners pair a
> **narrow workflow product** with a **media flywheel** and **agent execution**.
> The loop is **build audience → pre-sell → automate → repeat → compound**. End
> state Greg keeps quoting: vibe-code a SaaS to **~$50K/mo**, **~50%+ net margins**
> (no big team), then **sell for 10–15× EBITDA** → a **$3M–$5M** business.

## The core loop (ACP: Audience, Community, Product)

- **Line up buyers before you write code.** Audience first, product second.
- **Unbundle one killer feature** an incumbent does badly (Calendly = scheduling
  ripped out of bloated CRMs). One painful, recurring task, done perfectly.
- **Pick a sub-niche you understand** — ghostwriters, short-form editors,
  newsletter operators. Recurring, painful, time-consuming work = willingness to
  pay.
- **AI is the cofounder.** Launch with automation, not a team.

## The playbook steps (condensed from the 30)

### 1. Build a niche internet audience
- Dominate **one** platform (X / TikTok / LinkedIn) with **one** format.
- Faceless (AI-assisted) *or* personal brand — pick a lane.
- Plan for **linear, sustainable** growth; expect ~2 years of posting. No reliance
  on viral spikes.

### 2. Convert attention to a list
- At **~10K followers**, drop **lead magnets**: templates, mini-tools, e-books.
- **Community magnets** (Skool, WhatsApp) drive the deepest loyalty.
- Goal: own an **email list** so you're not renting the algorithm.

### 3. Build the wedge product
- Ship the **single feature**, vibe-coded, fast. A Loom demo → 5–10 contacts →
  pre-orders *before* polish.
- Bundle later: turn public micro-tools into **lead magnets + SEO assets**.

### 4. Price against subscription fatigue
- People are sick of subscriptions — test **lifetime deals** or **usage-based**
  pricing to cut friction and perceived risk.

### 5. Build an affiliate army (the flywheel's distribution)
- Recruit promoters with personalized (AI-scaled Loom) outreach.
- **40–50% commissions** for early top affiliates; **25–30%** for later partners;
  keep premium rates for original evangelists.

### 6. Reject VC, pursue a profitable exit
- **$2M ARR sells for $10M+ (5×+)**; skip the 10-year VC treadmill.
- Then **scale or exit** — sell and repeat, or compound into a portfolio.

## Marketing tips

- **Media is the moat.** Public micro-tools + content double as SEO assets and
  top-of-funnel lead magnets — distribution is built *into* the product line.
- **Pre-sell with a Loom + 5–10 DMs** before building. Validation = pre-orders,
  not "likes."
- **Lead magnet at 10K followers** is the trigger to start list-building; community
  (Skool/WhatsApp) out-retains a plain newsletter.
- **Affiliate ladder (40–50% → 25–30%)** turns customers into a sales force; AI
  scales the personalized outreach.
- **Pricing as marketing:** "lifetime / pay-per-use" is itself a wedge against
  subscription-fatigued buyers.

## Why this matters for us

- This *is* the launch-kit thesis spelled out: **idea validation (pre-sell) → lead
  funnel (magnet + list) → product**. Order matters — buyers before code.
- **Media flywheel = building marketable features that double as lead magnets.**
  Every public tool should pull SEO + email.
- The **$50K/mo → 50% margin → 10–15× exit** math is the concrete "why bother"
  framing for solo/small-team SaaS.
- **Affiliate ladder + pricing wedges** are copyable growth levers, numbers included.
