# The $1,000/hour Solo AI business (Full Course)

> Corey Ganim gives away his complete playbook for a solo AI services
> business: a **$999 "AI Tools Assessment"** for small business owners (45-min
> interview → prescribe 3–7 off-the-shelf tools → reclaim 5–10 h/week, full
> refund if less than 5 h found). No code, no audience, no capital. The
> assessment is a **tripwire**: ~50% of clients buy implementation after it
> (LTV $3–10K+), and the crown upsell is an **"AI Concierge" retainer** —
> two 45-min calls/month at $1,200–2,000 ≈ **$1,000/hour**. 15 assessments
> sold in 2026; concierge hit $8K MRR in 10 days (5 of first 6 prospects
> closed).

## The offer — AI Tools Assessment ($999)

- **ICP:** small business owners, 2–20 employees, $500K–5M revenue.
- **Promise:** 45-min structured interview, then a prescription of 3–7
  off-the-shelf AI/SaaS tools to reclaim **5–10 hours/week** (average client:
  7 h). **Guarantee: full refund if we can't find at least 5 h/week** →
  the only thing the client risks is 45 minutes.
- **Positioning:** "like a doctor writing a prescription" — you diagnose and
  prescribe existing tools; you build and implement *nothing* at this stage.
  That's why no coding skill is needed, only AI literacy.
- Why it works: owners are too busy to learn AI (40–60 h weeks), afraid of
  falling behind; ~95% of companies use nothing beyond prompting ChatGPT.
  Blue ocean by his own telling — he gives the playbook away because the
  market is millions of businesses.

## Fulfillment — four phases

1. **Discovery call (45 min, recorded).** AI note-taker (Fathom/Otter/
   Fireflies). Only probing, never prescribing: "Walk me through yesterday",
   "Which tasks do you dread?", "Where does work pile up?", "What did you try
   to automate and failed?", and the magic-wand question: *"If you could
   delete one process in your business, which?"* (surprisingly often: email).
   Discipline: bite your tongue — prescriptions come later.
2. **AI analysis.** Feed the transcript to Claude (he built a skill; a simple
   prompt works too: "here's the transcript, research off-the-shelf SaaS/AI
   tools that fix these pain points"). **QA step is human judgment**: Claude
   may prescribe Salesforce to a 4-person landscaper — sub in the
   small-business-appropriate tool. First 2–3 assessments the AI gets you
   60–70% there; after feeding finished reports back into the skill it's
   often copy-paste. Research directories: **futurepedia.io** (acquired by
   HubSpot) and **theresanaiforthat.com**, tagged by industry.
3. **The report (the deliverable — iterated 12×, built in Claude Design,
   before that Gamma).** Deliberately stupid-simple, because *"a confused
   mind doesn't buy, doesn't implement, doesn't get ROI, doesn't upsell."*
   Template slides: executive summary (1–2 pain points, hours reclaimable,
   primary focus) → **effort-vs-impact matrix** (top-left = quick wins =
   off-the-shelf tools; top-right = "major projects" = the upsell seed) →
   quick-win summary (pain-point one-liner → tool, e.g. "5 h/week in email →
   SaneBox") → recommended solutions (tool, pain point, cost, setup time,
   hours saved — 50% of the review call lives here) → **4-day quick-start
   plan** (one 5–10-min action/day against overwhelm-freeze) → "what comes
   after quick wins" (tees up implementation) → **financial-impact slide**
   (monthly net ROI = weekly hours × owner's hourly rate − tool cost;
   average tool spend ~$60/month → ROI always 4 figures, sometimes 5).
   Every tool must pull one of **three levers: effectiveness (more money),
   efficiency (time), quality**. Template giveaway: audittemplate.ai.
4. **Review call (~30 min).** Report emailed beforehand; screen-share and
   walk through. Three closing questions: *Which recommendation is most
   urgent? Do you want to do this yourself or with my help? What's your
   timeline?* → **50–60% ask for implementation.**

## Upsell menu ($999 is just the door; LTV $3–10K+)

1. **Process redesign** ($3–3.5K sold): fix the broken process *before*
   automating ("throw AI at a broken process and it falls apart") — map
   current state, deliver future-state blueprint; automation is a separate
   engagement.
2. **Automation builds** (~$1.5K): Zapier/Make/n8n for cut-and-dry
   input→output flows where a Claude skill is overkill (example: auto-
   duplicating Asana project templates for a wedding-venue business).
   That said: "90% of the time the answer is a Claude skill."
3. **Knowledge systems** (favorite): e.g. business broker drowning in 400–500
   identical buyer emails per listing → custom GPT trained on the marketing
   package, link instead of email → zero emails, delighted buyer AND seller.
   Pulls two levers at once (efficiency + quality).
4. **Custom workflows**: Claude skills + reference files for proprietary
   processes; train the team. Natural recurring component — processes change,
   so maintenance can be monthly/quarterly.
5. **Full implementation**: any combination, e.g. $8K package.
- Any upsell can become a **retainer** ("$3K/month for up to 2 knowledge
  systems", "$2K/month for up to 3 automations").
- **Favorite close:** credit the $999 assessment toward the upsell — after
  marking the upsell up by $1,000. Client feels the win, margin unchanged.

## Seven ways to find clients (zero capital, zero audience)

1. **Host a local "AI for business" meetup** — barter a co-working conference
   room ("I bring 30 local owners into your space"); 20-min talk on how to
   use Claude; collect contacts, follow up within 24 h offering the
   assessment. Works like SEO: compounds from event 3–6, not day 1. Combine
   with #5 (his first meetup, partnered with a plugged-in realtor, got him a
   client).
2. **Door knocking** — immediate results, nobody does it. Listener anecdote:
   30 doors → 5 meetings → 2 clients. "The single best way to learn sales."
3. **LinkedIn DMs** — local owners, probing not pitching in message one
   ("I probably live down the street from you — curious how you're using
   AI"). Voice notes/Looms raise response.
4. **Free mini-audits for your network** — "15 minutes, I'll show you one way
   AI benefits your business"; the full assessment is the upsell. Frame:
   worst case you learn one tool, best case you gain an AI partner.
5. **Agency/professional partnerships** — accountants, insurance agents,
   marketing agencies: their clients ask them about AI and they have no
   answer. Offer referral fee; the key is follow-up every 2–3 weeks.
   Co-branded workshops work too.
6. **AI office hours at a co-working space** — one free hour/week as the
   resident AI expert. Win-win-win; community member Dennis: 10 spaces
   pitched → 2 yes; door-knocked local shops for $175 in gift cards as show-up
   carrot → 11 attendees, 2 follow-up calls at office-hours #1.
7. **Post your wins** (build in public) — a "win" is smaller than you think
   (a good answer to a ChatGPT question counts); early on, manufacture value
   (e.g. 90 days of one useful prompt/day) until real client results arrive.

## The AI Concierge retainer (the $1,000/hour part)

- **Offer:** done-WITH-you consulting — two 45-min Zoom calls/month, client
  shares screen, you help them use **Claude Cowork** and build **Claude
  skills**. Plus unlimited Voxer access (12-business-hour response window) —
  sounds heavy, is nearly free: of 5 clients, 3 messaged once each in 2.5
  months. Very high perceived value, ~zero workload.
- **Pricing ladder he actually charged:** $1,200 → $1,500 → $1,500 → $1,800 →
  $2,000/month. $1,500 for 1.5 h ≈ $1,000/h. **Cap at 6 clients** — real
  scarcity, used openly on sales calls ("one seat left, two calls this
  week"). Greg's add-on: tiered "4 spots at $800, then $1,200" landing page.
- **Proof of demand:** told everyone he met; closed 5 of first 6; $8K MRR in
  10 days; kept raising price, people kept saying yes.
- **Session structure:** call #1 is pure foundation via a guided-onboarding
  Claude plugin (connect tools, context files, global instructions, show a
  scheduled task + a skill). Every later call: **AOA — Audit, Optimize,
  Automate** on one workflow at a time (show me how you do it → cut 13 steps
  to 7 → turn it into a Claude skill). Rinse, repeat.
- **Premium feel / retention:** pre-engagement Google Form ("what would make
  this a win in 90 days?" — 4 of 5 clients hit their 90-day win in ~60 days);
  a Notion one-pager per client with the running inventory of everything
  accomplished, emailed after every call — that's what renews the retainer.
- **Positioning advice (both):** niche by geography ("the AI guy in
  Charlotte") or by vertical you have real experience in ("AI assessments
  for financial services") — higher prices, less pricing pressure.

## Insights for me (Jens) — honest, not an echo

1. **This is the missing acquisition half of the KI-Sprint (waiting #75).**
   The sprint idea stalled on exactly what this episode solves: paid-search
   demand in DE is thin ("ki beratung" 2.4k/mo broad, CPC €6–12) and ads need
   budget. Corey's answer: **don't buy demand — harvest it locally at zero
   capital** (meetup, office hours, partnerships, network mini-audits). These
   channels don't appear in my 19-channel paid-first analysis and they fit
   Jens: Karlstein/Aschaffenburg has an IHK he already has a talk proposal
   for (pending since 2026-03), and the IHK *is* the German version of
   "co-working space full of ICP".
2. **The $999 assessment is a better wedge than the €3–4K sprint.** Lower
   friction, refund guarantee makes it near-riskless for the buyer, and it's
   *paid discovery* — the client pays you to scope the upsell. The existing
   ki-sprint landing page draft could gain an assessment tier without
   rebuilding anything. Sequence: Erstgespräch → €999 Assessment → Sprint as
   the "major projects" upsell. This also matches the 2026-04 pivot
   (AI automation = core business, Mittelstand track).
3. **Honest Taleb check: still a service, still an hours ceiling.** Not
   convex; it's Bahn-A cashflow, not the exit vehicle. But the concierge math
   changes the slope: 6 clients × €1,500 ≈ €9K MRR at ~9 h/month of calls —
   that alone would beat the short-term goal (5k/month) with the rest of the
   week free for product bets. The 100-day lesson ("value ≠ money yet")
   argues for exactly one cashflow lane like this.
4. **The report template = my Besichtigungsbogen pattern applied to B2B.**
   Executive summary + effort/impact matrix + 4-day quick start + ROI slide,
   iterated 12×. I already build these artifacts well (PDF pipeline). If Jens
   says go, the report template is a same-day build — no dependency on
   audittemplate.ai (unverified external).
5. **Fulfillment is literally my stack.** Transcript → Claude skill →
   researched tool list → templated report → review call. The "feed finished
   reports back into the skill" tip is the loop-engineering pattern from the
   13.07. episode applied to a service. Assessment fulfillment could run
   ~90% on the assistant.
6. **What I would NOT copy:** door knocking (not Jens), Voxer (Telegram
   exists), US pricing 1:1 (DE Mittelstand price sensitivity — start €749–999
   assessment, €1,200–1,500 concierge). And the "meetup" channel has a real
   German precedent risk: the local KI-Meetup Jens tried to attend in June
   was cancelled — hosting beats attending, but expect slow compounding
   (Corey says so himself: it's SEO, not door knocking).
