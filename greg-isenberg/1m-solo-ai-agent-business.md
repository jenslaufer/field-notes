# The $1M+ Solo AI Agent Business

> Nick from Orgo lays out the full A-to-Z for a one-person AI agent business
> that can clear a few million a year. The premise: **AI fluency itself is the
> moat** — ~99% of the market is behind, so anyone who can stand up Claude Code,
> Hermes, or OpenClaw has leverage businesses pay real money for. Headline
> pricing: **OpenClaw agents ~$5K/mo, Hermes agents ~$10K/mo**, sold as an
> "AI employee," not an "agent."

## The anti-friction offer

- **Sell unlimited, not units.** Unlimited agents, unlimited usage, unlimited
  monitoring, security, and ongoing changes for **~$5K/mo**. Removes friction
  and shortens time-to-yes.
- **Hide the plumbing.** No token-talk, no credit-talk — that breaks the magic.
  Flat retainer keeps the buyer focused on outcomes.
- **The reality gap that makes it profitable.** Customers *think* they need 10 or
  100 agents; in practice **1–3 agents** do the bulk of the work. You price for
  the fear, deliver against the reality.
- **Sell an AI employee, not an agent.** Frame it as a hire that works 24/7.
  Executives buy headcount and outcomes, not software categories.

## Picking the vertical

- **Avoid healthcare and finance** to start — too regulated for a solo operator.
- **Target legacy industries hungry to go AI-native:** marketing agencies, law
  firms, insurance agencies, manufacturers, wholesalers, real estate.
- **Look for executive-level pain that abstracts across companies** — the same
  workflow recurs at every firm in the vertical, so your build is reusable.

### Niche down with "diverge then converge"

- **Diverge first.** Try several verticals; see where the market pulls hardest.
- **Then converge by geography or professional type.** "Commercial real estate
  in Florida," "matrimonial law" — specificity makes a buyer feel personally
  addressed and lets you craft a sharper offer.

## The build stack

- **Customer-facing tools:** Granola, Trello, Loom, Superhuman, Asana.
- **Agent-building tools:** Codex, Hermes, Orgo, Composio, Agent Mail, Obsidian.
- **Obsidian = the second-brain layer.** Persistent knowledge/memory the agents
  read and write — the workspace's institutional memory.
- **Use agents to set up other agents.** Pair Claude Code or Codex with MCPs —
  **Perplexity, Context7, X MCP** — so the builder agent has live, current docs
  instead of stale training data.

### Model picks

- **GPT 5.5 — default for tool calling.** Efficient with tool calls; the OpenAI
  paid plan is generous on usage.
- **GLM 5.1 (Z.AI) — top open-source pick** for cheap, light tasks. **Kimmy**
  close behind.
- **Opus 4.7 — long-horizon coding** and complex multi-step builds.

## Live architecture (Orgo walkthrough)

- **One workspace per customer; one cloud computer per agent.** Clean isolation,
  easy to bill and monitor.
- **A Telegram-controlled meta-agent** installs Hermes, manages 27 VMs, and
  patches problems on the fly — an agent that manages other agents.
- **Cloud computers vs. Mac Minis** — cloud scales instantly per-agent; Mac
  Minis are a cheaper fixed-cost option for steady workloads.
- **Spawn parallel sub-agents** for research bursts, then collapse results back.
- **Reliability layer:** watchdogs + email-based observability so issues are
  caught before the customer feels them.

## Marketing tips

- **"Content is overpowered."** Nick's stated #1 customer-acquisition channel —
  publish the builds, demos, and results; let the work be the ad.
- **Lead with a 15-minute personal setup call** as the offer hook ("I'll
  personally set up your Hermes agent") — high-touch close that removes buyer
  risk and showcases competence live.
- **Sell the outcome in headcount terms** — "$5K/mo AI employee" beats "agent
  subscription" because it maps to a budget line execs already understand.
- **Niche the message, not just the product** — a Florida-CRE-specific landing
  pitch converts cold buyers far better than a generic "AI agents" pitch.
- **Demo the meta-agent.** Showing an agent that manages 27 VMs over Telegram is
  itself the proof-of-competence that closes legacy-industry skeptics.

## Why this matters for us

- **Unlimited-flat-retainer + hidden plumbing** is a clean monetization pattern:
  price for the buyer's fear, deliver against the 1–3-agent reality. Copyable for
  any AI service offer.
- **"Diverge then converge" on verticals** mirrors launch-kit idea-validation —
  run several offer×audience tests, then double down where the market pulls.
- **Content-as-acquisition + a free 15-min setup call** is a distribution-first
  funnel we can replicate: publish builds → capture leads → close on a live demo.
- **MCP-equipped builder agents (Perplexity/Context7/X MCP)** are a concrete
  recipe for keeping our own agent tooling current.
