# Marketing Playbook

> Every marketing/growth tip from the Greg Isenberg field notes, deduped and
> grouped. The through-line: **building is free, so distribution is the moat.**
> Marketers will be the wealthiest people of the next decade. Source episode in
> *(parentheses)*. Abbreviations explained in German: [`glossar.md`](glossar.md).

## 0. The one rule

- **Distribution first, product second — always.** Grow ~1,000 fans, ask what they
  need, build it in a weekend, launch to a *warm* audience. "If you build it they
  will come" is false. *(stop-vibe-coding, 23-ai-trends)*
- **Distribution is the real bottleneck, not the build.** The one-hour AI company
  only closes if you already have a list to sell the first 100 customers to.
  *(23-ai-trends)*

## 1. Cold-start: get an audience before you need one

- **Micro-monopoly:** a 100–5,000-person engaged niche audience is enough to launch.
  *(23-ai-trends)*
- **Can't grow one? Buy one.** A **10K-sub niche newsletter sells for $5K–$20K**;
  many owners make $0–$500/mo and will take a fair offer. You own the channel — a
  direct line, not a suppressible social account. *(stop-vibe-coding)*
- **Build in public when your followers are your customers** — they vote on the
  roadmap, become co-builders, trust compounds, you ship in 1–5 days. Community +
  speed is the moat. *(23-ai-trends)*
- **Shots on net.** With For You pages you never know which niche post pops —
  maximize volume over polish; you don't need many followers. *(stop-vibe-coding)*

## 2. Channels you can stand up this week

### SEO / programmatic SEO
- **Keyword-first, product-second.** Pick the keyword (volume × low KD × "near me"
  intent) *before* you build — the SEO opportunity *is* the GTM. *(online-directory)*
- **Programmatic SEO math to copy:** 10K pages × 30 visits/mo × 2% conv × $10 =
  **$60K/mo** from pages built once. Scrape data with Firecrawl → template →
  AI-generate *unique* paragraphs (not variable swaps) → human edit. Ship 100 as
  MVP, then scale. *(stop-vibe-coding, online-directory)*
- **Low CAC by design:** organic intent harvests demand instead of buying it;
  passive once indexed. *(online-directory)*

### AEO (Answer Engine Optimization) — be the source AI cites
- **AEO in 2026 = SEO in 2010.** First movers own niches for years. Peter Levels'
  AI referrals jumped **4% → 20% in one month**. *(stop-vibe-coding)*
- **Tactics:** structured direct answers, FAQ format, schema markup, parse-able
  comparison tables. Citation-worthy, not 3,000-word fluff. Monitor citations
  (Otterly / Profound). *(stop-vibe-coding)*

### MCP server as a zero-CAC sales channel
- **The AI assistant becomes your 24/7 sales team** — user asks an LLM → it
  discovers your MCP → returns your product. **Building an MCP in 2026 = mobile in
  2010.** One fintech case: 150+ installs in 30 days, $0 ad spend. Publish to
  Smithery / MCPT / Open Tools. *(stop-vibe-coding)*

### Free tool as top of funnel
- **The tool *is* the marketing** (Ahrefs model): instant value → email capture →
  user shares score → upsell. Run a **"free-tool calendar," ship one tool/week** —
  Claude Code makes it a day's work. *(stop-vibe-coding)*
- **Media is the moat:** public micro-tools + content double as SEO assets and lead
  magnets — distribution built *into* the product line. *(saas-minting)*

### Viral artifacts
- **Make your output shareable** — the artifact = what users brag about (Spotify
  Wrapped ~100M shares/yr, GitHub graph, Duolingo streak). Find the milestone they'd
  screenshot, make it beautiful + subtly branded, add a pre-filled share button.
  Works in B2B (Slack/Teams shares too). *(stop-vibe-coding)*

### Short-form social (Reels / TikTok)
- **Steal structure, not content.** Feed a proven viral clip to Manus → get the
  mechanics → rebuild in your niche. Reverse-engineer creators in the **50K–200K**
  range (big enough to have hits, niche enough to transfer). *(viral-ig-reels)*
- **One "impossible shot" per Reel** (SeaDance) as the scroll-stopper in the first
  15s — perceived production jump drives saves/shares. *(viral-ig-reels)*
- **Slideshow > talking-head** for a faceless product account — cheap, variable,
  native. *(openclaw-go-viral)*
- **Curiosity hooks beat insult hooks.** Reveal the payoff *first*, explain second
  ("I showed my mom what AI thinks our living room could look like"). *(openclaw-go-viral)*
- **Lean into "mistakes"** — an obvious glitch comment-baits and lifts reach.
  *(openclaw-go-viral)*
- **Soft "Part 2" CTA**, not a hard sell — trains the algorithm on re-engagement and
  seeds a series. *(viral-ig-reels)*
- **Drafts-over-API trick:** upload via API to *Drafts*, then add a **trending sound**
  manually on mobile and publish by hand — dodges bot-flagging, unlocks sounds the
  API can't touch, signals authenticity. *(openclaw-go-viral)*
- **Instagram is the funnel + social-proof engine** — treat the IG account as the
  storefront, not the App Store page. Manufacture social proof visibly. *(making-money-with-ios-apps)*

## 3. Paid ads & creative

- **Volume over polish.** Launch **5–15 creatives** per test; let the platform find
  the winning hook. *(making-money-with-ios-apps)*
- **Ugly ads that name the pain pull ~$1–$1.50; great creative gets to $3.**
  Generate 1,000 ugly variations cheaply to find the winning *angle*, then spend
  tokens (Nano Banana Pro / Kie.ai) to remix the winner. *(145k-marketing-machine)*
- **One winning format ports everywhere:** static → UGC video via HeyGen API → bulk
  upload. *(145k-marketing-machine)*
- **Steal frameworks from DTC e-com** (highest spend = best hooks); mine the
  Facebook Ad Library, repurpose for boring/B2B. *(ai-marketing-masterclass)*
- **Statics over AI-UGC for now** — extract frameworks, generate a few statics
  (Nano Banana Pro via Glyph MCP); Remotion for product-demo videos. *(ai-marketing-masterclass)*
- **Influencers: target ~$2 CPM**, go wide on small/mid niche creators; clips double
  as ad creative + IG social proof (one shoot, three uses). *(making-money-with-ios-apps)*
- **Scale, don't optimize.** Run **5 campaigns/month instead of 1**, ship many
  variations vs. refreshing every 6 weeks. *(450k-campaign)*

## 4. Positioning & messaging

- **Verticalize the message, not just the product.** "Computer-use agent for
  distributorships" / "CRM for Florida CRE" beats generic "AI agents." Borrow
  a16z's vertical thesis. *(making-money-with-openclaw, 1m-solo-agent, firecrawl)*
- **Compete on niche, not features** — do one thing perfectly for one customer at
  $20–$70/mo under the horizontal giant. *(firecrawl)*
- **Sell the outcome in headcount terms** — "$5K/mo AI employee" beats "agent
  subscription" because it maps to a budget line execs already understand. *(1m-solo-agent)*
- **Lead with the prospect's loss, not your feature** — the winning landing variant
  named the buyer's failure ("a lost deal," "an objection your rep wasn't ready
  for"). *(ab-test-startups)*
- **Mine real pain** from Reddit/YouTube/Twitter via Perplexity — sell the outcome,
  don't invent copy. *(145k-marketing-machine)*
- **"We run 100× more tests" is the whole pitch** — lead with the number, not the
  tech. *(karpathy-autoresearch)*
- **Credibility is the conversion moat** when anyone can ship features — invest in
  proof and reputation. *(ai-agents-run-my-business, design-with-weavy)*

## 5. Pricing as marketing

- **Charge per result / per batch.** Reads as ROI, not a SaaS subscription — easier
  yes, fatter margins. Being **first-mover on outcome pricing is itself a sales
  hook**. *(firecrawl, 23-ai-trends)*
- **Performance pricing closes deals** — retainer + bonus on a KPI lift / rev-share
  aligns upside. *(karpathy-autoresearch)*
- **"Lifetime / pay-per-use" as a wedge** against subscription-fatigued buyers.
  *(saas-minting)*
- **Replace the price table with an ROI calculator** when pain is quantifiable —
  interaction beats a static table for B2B capture. *(ab-test-startups)*
- **Lead the niche on price transparency** where competitors hide pricing — user
  hook *and* search differentiator. *(online-directory)*

## 6. Lead magnets & funnels

- **Make lead magnets without writing an ebook:** record yourself working 2h →
  transcribe → Claude Code extracts a playbook/guide. *(ai-marketing-masterclass, 450k-campaign)*
- **Provocative self-assessment magnets convert** — e.g. "Cave Dweller Audit: are
  you building or promoting?" exposes the buyer's pain. *(450k-campaign)*
- **One angle = one landing page.** Generate a *separate* dialed page per ICP →
  higher conversion than one generic page. "Become a testing machine — 100 landing
  pages, 100 ads." *(ai-marketing-masterclass)*
- **Test the headline first** — highest-leverage variable; swap per segment with no
  redeploy, learn in hours. *(ab-test-startups)*
- **Onboarding *is* the sales page** — its only job is to reach the paywall with
  intent. Optimize it like a funnel, not a tutorial. *(making-money-with-ios-apps)*
- **"Optimize" button as a campaign:** email the list *"press this, watch it improve
  itself"* — the magic-moment demo doubles as the Pro upsell. *(karpathy-autoresearch)*

## 7. Sales & offer mechanics

- **Lead with a working demo, not a pitch** — build the proof first, attach it to
  the proposal; demo closes faster than copy. *(making-money-with-openclaw, 1m-solo-agent)*
- **15-minute personal setup call as the hook** ("I'll set up your agent
  personally") — high-touch close that removes risk and shows competence live.
  *(1m-solo-agent)*
- **Outreach is the product:** personalized email referencing each prospect's own
  job post — relevance at scale (14 tailored emails from 222 scraped jobs).
  Trigger-event targeting (fresh hire, funding, closure) beats spray-and-pray.
  *(start-ai-agent-business)*
- **Upwork as paid lead-gen** — buyers with budget and a written brief; reverse-
  engineer the demand. *(making-money-with-openclaw)*
- **Pre-sell with a Loom + 5–10 DMs** before building. Validation = pre-orders, not
  likes. *(saas-minting)*
- **Productize the wedge into a reusable skill** — once one client's workflow works,
  it resells across the vertical at near-zero marginal cost. The build is cheap; the
  **packaging is the margin** ($5K–$10K/mo managed service). *(making-money-with-openclaw, ab-test-startups)*
- **Affiliate ladder (40–50% → 25–30%)** turns customers into a sales force; AI
  scales the personalized outreach. *(saas-minting)*

## 8. Content engine (own voice, no slop)

- **"Content is overpowered"** — Nick's #1 acquisition channel: publish the builds,
  demos, and results; let the work be the ad. *(1m-solo-agent)*
- **Ride your own obsessions.** Surface the themes you've circled for years — your
  most authentic, differentiated angles. *(obsidian-personal-os)*
- **Vault → content pipeline:** convert private notes into a backlog of essays /
  threads, drafted in *your* voice so it reads like you, not AI slop. *(obsidian-personal-os)*
- **Research what's working *this month*, then copy it** — pull current cold-email
  frameworks / trending formats live, not 2023 advice. *(last-30-days-skill)*
- **Engagement = demand signal** — a 1,500-upvote thread is validation; rank ideas
  by reactions, not editorial coverage. *(last-30-days-skill)*
- **Ride the named-credibility wave** — be the first clear explainer of a
  viral-but-foggy tool ("in the fog, people don't see the opportunity"). *(karpathy-autoresearch)*
- **One pillar → 7 channels:** a 30-min voice memo → tweets, LinkedIn, shorts,
  newsletter, quote graphics. The value is in the repurposing. *(stop-vibe-coding)*

## 9. Design & brand as conversion

- **Taste is the differentiator, not the model** — when everyone has the same AI,
  the on-brand page converts and gets remembered. *(ai-design-no-slop, design-with-weavy)*
- **Lead with feeling, then features** — decide the emotion before the UI; that's
  what makes an app shareable vs. forgettable. *(design-with-weavy)*
- **Reference, don't describe** — hand the agent real HTML/visual references (Cosmos
  / Are.na moodboard) instead of adjectives. *(ai-design-no-slop, design-with-weavy)*
- **Distinct color + logo + buttons = trust** — branded surfaces signal "real
  product," lifting perceived quality and conversion over template apps. *(design-with-weavy)*
- **Ship custom across every surface** (page, slides, motion, mobile) from one
  DESIGN.md so the brand reads consistent everywhere a prospect lands. *(ai-design-no-slop)*

## 10. The "gotcha feature" + metrics that decide it

- **Make the feature marketable, not just useful** — pick the one thing you can show
  in a 3-second clip that makes a stranger say "wait, how?" If you can't make the
  clip, you don't have the feature. Reverse-engineer what's already going viral.
  *(making-money-with-ios-apps)*
- **The numbers the app math hinges on:** **$2 ARPU target**, **~$100/day** ad test
  budget, kill losers fast. The model only works when **paid CAC < ARPU ×
  retention** — a 300K-view hook means nothing if it doesn't sell. **Optimize to
  revenue, not vanity** (put RevenueCat in the loop). *(making-money-with-ios-apps, openclaw-go-viral)*
- **Honest caveat:** viral installs ≠ retained subs. Churn is high; fix onboarding
  or the loop just rents users. *(openclaw-go-viral)*

## Why this matters for us

- **Launch-kit is a distribution-first bet** — these tactics (warm-list validation,
  free-tool + email capture, per-result pricing, one-angle-per-ICP landing pages)
  are directly copyable into the idea-validation funnel.
- **The cheapest copyable channels for us:** programmatic SEO + AEO (Firecrawl +
  Claude Code), a weekly free tool, and an MCP server — all zero/low-CAC.
- **Pricing and positioning are marketing** — verticalized message + outcome/
  per-result pricing is a higher-converting wedge than generic SaaS framing.
