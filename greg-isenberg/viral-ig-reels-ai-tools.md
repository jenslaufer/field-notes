# AI Tools (Manus, FreePik) for Viral IG Reels

> Cova racks up millions of Reels views with an AI-assisted visual style. The
> workflow isn't "make AI videos" — it's **reverse-engineer what already went
> viral**, rebuild it with a repeatable 5-act structure, then enhance footage
> with AI so it reads as high-production. The pipeline is **Manus → FreePik
> Nanobanana → SeaDance → CapCut/Premiere → Obsidian + Claude Code**.

## The tool stack (in order of use)

- **Manus** — browser agent that *watches a viral Reel frame-by-frame* and
  outputs a replication plan: transition timings, color grade, script triggers,
  visual + audio mechanics. Analyzes captions *and* pixels, not guesses.
- **FreePik (Nanobanana)** — AI image editor. Annotate a single frame to add
  environment (fairy lights, bookshelves, vintage tech). Background enhancement.
- **SeaDance (Pro)** — video / "impossible shots": a photo that comes alive, a
  camera move that zooms *through* an object. Manufactures the aha-moment.
- **CapCut / Adobe Premiere** — primary edit + assembly.
- **Descript** — screen recordings for process B-roll.
- **Obsidian + Claude Code** — the content *system*: index every Manus
  deconstruction, build a personal style guide, auto-convert script → storyboard.

## The 5-act structure (the reusable template)

- **1. Hook** — show the *finished result* immediately, bold title card. Visual
  first, words second.
- **2. Conflict** — a personal/technical problem to create stakes + relatability.
- **3. Build** — three distinct steps, circular number overlays, fast B-roll.
- **4. Problem → resolution** — show a failure *before* the win (three-beat arc).
- **5. Tease + CTA** — lowercase casual caption ending in a "Part 2" hook.

## Hands-on production rules

- **First 15 seconds decide everything.** The concept must be explainable in
  <10 seconds with a visual payoff you can show instantly.
- **Concept hook in 2–3 seconds**, or the scroll wins.
- **Talking-head setup:** dark room at night, blackout curtains, RGB lighting,
  multiple visible monitors (signals "builder"), dark solid clothing.
- **Three B-roll types:** portrait macro (textures), process shots (Descript
  screen-record), product reveal (natural light contrast).
- **FreePik rule:** keep AI overlays *away from body movement* — added elements
  on moving limbs break the illusion. Enhance static zones only.

## Marketing tips

- **Steal structure, not content.** Feed a proven viral Reel to Manus, get the
  exact mechanics, rebuild in your niche. You're copying the *why it worked*.
- **Reverse-engineer creators in the 50K–200K range** — big enough to have hits,
  niche enough that the pattern transfers to you.
- **Manufacture one "impossible shot"** per Reel (SeaDance) as the scroll-stopper
  in the first 15s — the perceived production jump drives saves/shares.
- **CTA = soft "Part 2" hook**, not a hard sell. Trains the algorithm on
  re-engagement and seeds a series.
- **Systematize with Obsidian + Claude Code** so you ship volume on-style instead
  of reinventing each video — consistency compounds the algorithm's trust.

## Why this matters for us

- **Distribution-first, productized:** a repeatable content *system* (deconstruct
  → template → enhance → ship) beats one-off "content." Maps to building a media
  flywheel around launch-kit.
- The **5-act structure + 15-second rule** are copyable for any product demo or
  feature-launch short.
- **AI as enhancement layer, not the whole video** — credibility comes from real
  footage with surgical AI polish. A guardrail for our own marketing clips.
