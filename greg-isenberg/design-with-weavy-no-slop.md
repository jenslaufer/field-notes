# Stop Shipping AI Slop — Design with Weavy AI + Claude

> Suraya Shivji (sold her last company to Snap) names the core flaw in vibe
> coding: "Most people vibe code what it does. Nobody thinks about how it should
> make someone feel. That's why everything looks the same." Live, she turns a
> generic AI-Studio prototype of a **voice journaling app** into a branded one
> by layering brand strategy, a real moodboard, and Weavy-generated assets — then
> feeding the design back in as a reference.

## The thesis: "slop" is a feeling problem
- **Slop = generic, interchangeable design** that screams amateur no matter how
  clean the code is.
- **Vibe coders optimize function, skip feeling.** Brand is the missing layer.
- **The fix is intentional brand → assets → reference**, not better prompts.

## The full workflow (generic prototype → branded app)
1. **Prototype fast** — generate the basic voice-journaling app in **Google AI Studio**.
2. **Capture it** — screenshot the screens, import into **Figma** as the working canvas.
3. **Write brand guidelines with Claude** — strategy first: who it's for, the
   feeling, tone, personality. This is the step most people skip.
4. **Build a moodboard in Cosmos** — pull visual direction (also Pinterest / Are.na)
   so the brand has a real reference, not a vibe.
5. **Generate assets in Weavy AI** — import the moodboard images, then use AI
   models to produce **color palettes, buttons, logos, icons** that match the brand.
6. **Assemble in Figma** — bring Weavy's elements back and compose the real design.
7. **Refine the app with the design as reference** — feed the finished design back
   into Google AI Studio so the build matches the brand, not the default template.

## Stack (and the on-ramp)
- **Beginner stack**: Claude + **Weavy AI** + Figma.
- **Add as you level up**: **Google Stitch** + **Cosmos** (moodboards).
- **Weavy = the translation layer** — turns moodboard + brand guidelines into
  tangible, reusable design elements. (Weavy is now **Figma Weave**.)

## Marketing tips
- **Lead with feeling, then features.** Decide the emotion before the UI; that
  emotional fit is what makes an app memorable and shareable vs. forgettable slop.
- **Brand guidelines as a Claude artifact** — a written strategy doc (audience,
  tone, personality) becomes a reusable conversion asset across app, landing page, ads.
- **Moodboard before pixels.** Cosmos / Pinterest / Are.na give the AI a concrete
  visual target, so generated assets feel curated, not random.
- **Distinct color + logo + buttons = trust.** Branded surfaces signal "real
  product," lifting perceived quality and conversion over default-template apps.
- **Reference-driven refinement**: feeding the finished design back into the
  builder keeps every new screen on-brand — consistency compounds.

## Why this matters for us
- **Direct anti-slop playbook for our UI**: brand strategy → moodboard → Weavy
  assets → reference back into the build. Copyable for any product surface.
- **The "how should it feel" question** is a cheap, high-leverage gate before we
  ship any landing page or app screen.
- **Weavy/Figma Weave** is worth trialing as the asset layer between Claude/Stitch
  and our Tailwind builds.
- **Branding lifts conversion**, not just looks — a distribution argument for
  investing design time early in the launch-kit.
