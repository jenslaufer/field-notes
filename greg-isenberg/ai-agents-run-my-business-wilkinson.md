# AI Agents Run My Business and Life

> Andrew Wilkinson restructured his work, health, and **family office** around AI
> agents. He vibe-coded **Deep Personality** (a SaaS doing ~$20K rev run almost
> entirely by agents), runs autonomous ops through the **Harbor** harness, and
> queries Tiny + his holding company through a **vector database** like an oracle.
> His CFO — zero coding background — vibe-coded a replacement for **Adapar**
> ($50K–$100K/yr) in ~2 weeks. The office swapped headcount for a **$40K/mo Claude
> bill**. Best prompting tip: **make the model interview you first**.

## The Claude Code unlock

- **The December 2025 moment:** waking at 3–4am with ten terminal tabs open,
  running a whole SaaS business from the back of Ubers in Arizona, laptop-free.
- **The hook is real, the debugging tax is real** — Deep Personality makes ~$20K
  but debugging eats half his time.
- **Claude Code as operating system** — Andrew standardized his entire workflow
  on it.

## Harbor: an agent harness for real companies

- **Built by Gavin Vickery** (github.com/geekforbrains/Harbor) — a GUI-style
  harness giving agents a structured workspace.
- **Dev, marketing, and support agents share one knowledge base.**
- **They act autonomously:** auto-merge P0 security fixes, merge PRs, run
  multivariate ad tests, adjust ad budgets across **PostHog, Meta, Reddit**.
- **Andrew's read:** support-as-a-job is months from disappearing; marketing
  agents will reshape what a $100K/mo ad budget even looks like.

## Reality check on autonomous companies

- **Today's agents = "Zapier zaps with intelligence."** Capable but need
  step-by-step instruction — "a genius baby."
- **The real unlock arrives at 5–10M-token context** — when a model can hold a
  whole company in mind at once.
- **Credibility is the missing layer** for vibe-coded products — trust, not
  features, is what's hard to fake.

## Centralizing context: G-Brain + vector DBs

- **G-Brain** (Gary Tan's vibe-coded vector-database tool) ingests Fireflies
  transcripts, emails, and meeting notes **nightly**.
- **Trained on the family office:** surfaces **$16M invested → $36M current value
  across 132 deals**; query P&Ls, headcount, and 132 minority investments
  conversationally.
- **Trained on Tiny:** acts as the **"eye of Sauron"** across 24 portfolio
  companies.
- **Centralize data pipelines first** — the oracle is only as good as the feed.

## The family-office restructure

- **Headcount → a $40K/mo Claude bill.** Agents replaced roles.
- **CFO with zero coding vibe-coded an Adapar replacement** (a wealth/net-worth
  platform priced $50K–$100K/yr) in **~2 weeks**.
- **"Services as the new software"** — bespoke internal tools beat buying SaaS
  when an agent can build exactly what you need.

## Daily AI workflows (life)

- **Health:** ran psychological screens on himself and his girlfriend → fed them
  into Deep Personality.
- **Email triage** and a **personalized morning podcast** generated daily.
- The same agent stack runs business and personal life.

## Prompting tactics

- **Make the model interview you first** — ask it to pose **multiple-choice
  questions** before generating any output. Best tip in the episode.
- **Give agents a shared knowledge base** so dev/marketing/support stay consistent.
- **Pipe everything into a vector DB nightly** so context compounds instead of
  scattering across tools.

## Marketing tips

- **Marketing agents as the new ad team** — multivariate ad tests + autonomous
  budget shifts across PostHog/Meta/Reddit; rethink what a $100K/mo budget buys.
- **Credibility is the conversion moat** for vibe-coded products — when anyone can
  ship features, *trust* is the differentiator. Invest in proof and reputation.
- **Personalized morning podcast** as a retention/attention pattern — bespoke
  daily audio is a sticky, copyable content format.
- **Multiple-choice interview prompt** doubles as a marketing-copy generator —
  let the model extract your positioning before it writes.

## Why this matters for us

- **"Interview me with multiple-choice questions first"** is an immediately
  copyable prompt pattern for any generation task — improves our own agent outputs.
- **Services-as-software**: vibe-coding bespoke internal tools (vs. buying SaaS)
  reframes build-vs-buy for our stack.
- **Credibility-as-moat** sharpens distribution-first thinking — when building is
  free, trust and proof are the marketable edge.
- **Nightly vector-DB ingestion** is a concrete architecture for a queryable
  second brain over our own data.
