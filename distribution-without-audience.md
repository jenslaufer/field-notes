# Distribution without an audience

> For a builder with **no audience** who finds audience-building too high-commitment.
> Core insight: **"distribution first" ≠ "audience first."** Audience is just *one*
> channel — the slowest and most identity-binding one. You can win distribution by
> **harvesting existing demand** instead of manufacturing it.
> Abbreviations explained in German: [`greg-isenberg/glossar.md`](greg-isenberg/glossar.md).

## The reframe

"You need an audience" is survivorship-biased advice from people whose business *is*
audience (Greg, Levels). Three distinct things get conflated:

1. **Personal brand / creator audience** — high commitment, public, identity-linked,
   multi-year payback. *This* is what feels risky, and that instinct is correct.
2. **Owned email list** — you deliver value, you don't *perform*. Medium effort, no
   persona, compounds. Much lower commitment than a personal brand.
3. **Demand-harvesting channels** — SEO, marketplaces, paid, outreach, free tools.
   No face, no posting cadence, no identity bet. You catch demand that already exists.

The real trap isn't "no personal brand." It's building **no distribution at all** —
then every launch is a cold start and you buy each customer one at a time forever.

**Rule:** pick commitment-light channels; let an owned list grow as a *byproduct* of
work you already do (e.g. these field notes).

## Channels ranked — most traction × fastest feedback first

Optimized for a no-audience builder validating offer × audience quickly.

| # | Channel | Feedback | Traction | Commitment | $ cost | Use when |
|---|---------|----------|----------|------------|--------|----------|
| 1 | **Paid ads → landing page** | Hours–days | High | Low | Pays per click | You have an offer + a page and want a verdict *now*. Fastest signal money can buy. |
| 2 | **Pre-sell: Loom + 10–20 targeted DMs/emails** | Days | Medium | Low | ~Free | Pre-revenue. Tests willingness to *pay*, not "likes". Validation = pre-orders. |
| 3 | **Trigger-event cold outreach** (scraped, personalized) | Days | Medium–High | Low | ~Free + tools | B2B. Sell into companies at the moment budget appears (new hire, funding, closure). |
| 4 | **Marketplaces / launch platforms** (App Store, Product Hunt, directories, MCP registries) | Days–weeks | Medium–High | Low | ~Free | Distribution is built into the channel. Ship where buyers already browse. |
| 5 | **Free tool as top-of-funnel** | Days–weeks | Medium, compounds | Low–Med | Cheap | Ship a tool in a day → instant value → email capture → upsell. Run a tool/week. |
| 6 | **Programmatic SEO / AEO** (Google- + KI-Such-Optimierung) | Weeks–months | High, compounds | Low | Cheap | Long game. Slow feedback, near-zero acquisition cost once indexed. Keyword-first, product-second. |
| 7 | **Owned email list** (byproduct) | Slow to build, then fast | High, compounds | Medium | ~Free | The asset that makes *every* future launch warm. Deliver value, don't perform. |
| — | ~~Personal brand / creator audience~~ | Months–years | High | **High** | Time | Skip unless you genuinely enjoy it. Highest commitment, slowest payback. |

### How to read the table
- **Top of list (1–3) = fastest verdict.** Use these to validate an idea this week.
- **Middle (4–5) = real traction, still low commitment.** Build these once an offer survives validation.
- **Bottom (6–7) = compounding assets.** Slow feedback, but they lower CAC for years.
  Start the list early *because* it's slow — it's the only channel that pays back the cold-start cost.
  (CAC = Customer Acquisition Cost = Kosten, einen Kunden zu gewinnen.)

## The play for a multi-product builder

1. **Validate with paid + outreach** (rows 1–3) — get a yes/no on offer × audience in days, no audience required.
2. **Win traction with marketplaces + a free tool** (rows 4–5) — distribution baked into the channel.
3. **Compound with SEO + an owned list** (rows 6–7) — the long-term moat, grown as a byproduct.
4. **Content only as a byproduct** of building (these notes, build logs) — "build in public lite" without the identity bet.

> Honest caveat: skipping audience means you pay for early distribution (ads, time)
> instead of borrowing it. That's a fair trade *if* you start the compounding
> channels (SEO + list) early, so you're not renting attention forever.
