# Tim Soulo of Ahrefs — Massive shifts in SEO, AEO, plus what content works

> The CMO of a **$100M+/year, bootstrapped, no-touch (zero-sales-call) business**
> on how search is breaking and what survives. The whole interview is a distribution
> masterclass. Core thesis: forget "SEO" — the durable thing is being **discoverable**.
> The biggest, most actionable idea: **half of all Google searches are *branded*, and
> branded search does not originate in Google.** You make people notice you out in the
> world; *then* they search for you; *then* SEO/AEO serves that search and grows it.
> Channels and algorithms change — the principle doesn't.

## The shift: "gradually, then suddenly"
- Google ate clicks for years (featured snippets, knowledge panels, "people also ask",
  lyrics) — killing whole content categories. Now **AI overviews + ChatGPT search**
  make it sudden. Biggest change Soulo has seen, full stop.
- **Evergreen topics are dying for SEO.** "How many copywriting guides can you write?"
  Everyone regurgitates the same keywords; Google can amalgamate 10–20 years of it into
  its own AI answer and never rank you. **Bet on NEW topics instead** — F1 2026 rules,
  "Ahrefs MCP" — where no info exists yet. Be first, get the search traffic; copycats
  then pile in with AI. SEO still works, but at the *front* of a trend, not the middle.

## "Discoverable" — the principle that survives (his book's pivot)
Platforms and trends change; the principles of being discoverable stay the same. Two ways:
1. **When people search, they find you** — understand what people search *and* how the
   answer engine (Google / ChatGPT / Gemini) generates its answer. "People will not stop
   searching" — it's still a search even when the surface changes.
2. **Get noticed** — do something that sticks out, something unique/hard most won't do.
   You don't get noticed typing words alone in a room.

## Branded search is the whole game (the centerpiece)
- **~Half of all Google searches are branded** (Ahrefs measured it). Brands = products,
  people, movies, companies ("how do I reset my iPhone" is branded).
- **Branded search originates OFF Google.** Something compels you to search — you heard
  about the brand on social, YouTube, TV. So: **social media / real-world stunts CREATE
  branded search; SEO/AEO SERVES it.** They're not competitors, they're a relay.
- **Fulfilling a branded search GROWS it.** Ahrefs saw 10–15/mo searches for a "Data
  Studio connector", built it + a landing page → people found it, used it, told others →
  search volume climbed. Same with a "how to pronounce Ahrefs" video (crowdsourced from
  LinkedIn, now ranks #1, people send it to each other). *Serve the tiny branded searches
  you already have — it compounds.*
- **Chase "Neville + copywriting", not "copywriting".** Ranking for a generic head term =
  renting the algorithm; the day you drop, the traffic stops. A branded search means they
  find you anywhere — YouTube, TikTok, wherever. Soulo: "I missed this for 10+ years."

## Get noticed = do something real, then document it
- Neville got homeless people to sell bottled water on the roadside, documented it →
  referenced to this day, met countless people. Beats "I sold my monitor on eBay."
- My First Million's **clip contest**: "$5k to anyone whose podcast clip hits 100k views"
  → hundreds of millions of views, flood of new listeners. ~**200x ROI**.
- "Hire yourself to Ahrefs" — Soulo flipped recruiting into a 3-question form ("what does
  Ahrefs suck at?") → 200+ applicants, huge buzz, hired someone with *no job opening*.
- **AI cannot do crazy stuff in the real world and write about it.** That's the moat for
  human content now — first-hand experience and stunts, not regurgitated how-tos.

## Engines, not campaigns
- Ahrefs barely used the word "campaign" until it started paid ads. A campaign = a budget,
  banners, a burst. What they actually built were **long-lasting engines**: the blog, the
  YouTube channel (biggest SEO channel on YouTube), data-research studies. An engine
  compounds for years; a campaign ends. *Build engines.*

## How Ahrefs *actually* grew (the unglamorous truth)
- **"Free" was the word.** (1) A backlink tool visibly better than rivals (daily-updated
  DB vs monthly). (2) **Instant free report, no signup** on the homepage. (3) Free access
  gifted to industry big names → they evangelized.
- **Freemium → cheap trial:** at one point **8k paying customers and 16k people on the
  free trial** (people abused a $7/7-day trial endlessly). They didn't care — those 16k
  experienced full power, became the biggest advocates, and flooded Reddit with "use
  Ahrefs". Without them the online buzz would've been 3x smaller.
- **$100M+ ARR, all inbound, no-touch, bootstrapped.** Tiny sales team handles only
  *incoming* enterprise requests; no warm outbound at all. "It's all connected" — content
  educates → word of mouth → branded search → signup. Remove the educating content and
  word of mouth dies.
- **They killed their affiliate program.** Top 3 affiliates = 90% of affiliate revenue,
  and all of them ran **free SEO tools** (free backlink checker) with an Ahrefs link.
  Ahrefs decided: those are competitors for our traffic — *we'll build our own free tools
  and outrank them.* (Plus affiliates need babysitting + incentive to oversell.) Replaced
  with one-off paid **partnerships** (disclosed) → "every good Ahrefs review you see is
  genuine."

## AEO — Answer Engine Optimization (the new layer)
- For classic SEO, **links** are still the #1 ranking factor.
- For AI answers, the factor is **brand mentions + product mentions** — the more other
  sites mention your brand/product favorably, the more you appear in ChatGPT/Gemini. Same
  logic as links (others vouching for you), new currency. People may not click, but they
  *learn your brand exists* — which feeds branded search again.
- YouTube and Reddit are huge citation sources for AI answers; **individual LinkedIn/X
  posts generate almost no search traffic** (don't confuse posting with SEO).
- Ahrefs' fastest-growing product ever is **Brand Radar** (shows how often you're cited
  across AI engines) — tells you where this is all heading.

## AI + taste (the operator notes)
- **AI = infinite variations; taste = picking the good one.** Rick Rubin can't play an
  instrument — he's paid for confident taste in what's good. Same with AI output.
- **Vibe-coding wins for engineers** (models are tuned for code). **For marketing it's
  trickier** — word order changes the effect — so human judgment matters more.
- **Soulo's podcast-prep skill:** drop a guest's LinkedIn URL → AI finds their recent
  interviews, de-dupes (talk vs interview), TL;DRs each, cross-references for *unique*
  takes, merges all intros into one. Teach it by **examples** (feed it old prep notes +
  the actual interviews so it learns your style); after each edit, tell it to *update the
  skill* from your edits → it gets better every time.
- **⚠️ Calculators are getting invalidated.** Neville: ChatGPT now makes a *personalized*
  pricing calculator/spreadsheet on the fly → "the whole idea of a generic calculator is
  mostly invalidated." Soulo's pushback: not everyone has good ideas or knows the micro-
  decisions, so there's still room — but the warning is real for any generic-tool play.

## "Marketing is not math" (his analytics skepticism)
- You *can* track (e.g. scrape who engages your LinkedIn posts, cross-ref to signups) —
  "but then what?" Knowing a channel works doesn't tell you do-more / do-less / do-same.
- Too many other variables decide strategy: am I good at this channel? do I have time?
  do I even have ideas for it? **He deliberately under-weights attribution numbers.**
- On focus vs spread: he just **posts everywhere** — low hit rate, but people find him on
  TikTok/LinkedIn/YouTube he forgot he was even on. Anecdotal beats false precision.

## Insights for distribution (the direct answer for us)
1. **Brand before search.** Our products have no brand → no branded search → we lean on
   generic SEO, which is *exactly* the channel that's dying (FK/HC = evergreen YMYL that
   Google now answers itself). This finally explains *why* FK/HC are traffic-dead beyond
   "young domain": they target amalgamatable evergreen terms with no brand pulling people.
2. **Free, instant, no-signup tools are a proven traction engine — and Jens already has
   the asset.** Ahrefs bootstrapped on a free instant report; their best affiliates were
   *free tools*. This is a direct endorsement of **embeddable mini-tools (channel #4)** and
   **fingrab**. The concept-simulators and calculators are the right *shape* — what's
   missing is the brand/notice layer that turns a user into a searcher.
3. **Serve the tiny branded searches you have.** fingrab already has installs + 3 paying
   subs + a name people type. Make sure "fingrab" the query is perfectly served (store
   listing, a "how to use fingrab" page) — fulfilling it grows it via word of mouth. Same
   for "Jens Laufer".
4. **Get noticed by doing one real, slightly-crazy, documented thing** — not by posting
   nicely. This is the same conclusion as [`distribution-without-audience.md`](../distribution-without-audience.md)
   and the Greg-Isenberg "skill 2" thread, now confirmed by a $100M operator. Stunt → notice
   → branded search → SEO/AEO serves it.
5. **Build engines, not campaigns.** Direct cross-check on the **KI-Sprint paid-search**
   debate (waiting #75): a paid campaign is a burst with a hard ceiling; an engine
   (concept-sim family, a real blog, a free tool) compounds. Bias the runway toward engines.
6. **Optimize for being *mentioned*, not just ranked (AEO).** For solytics/e-invoice the
   future lever is brand/product mentions across the web so we surface in AI answers about
   e-Rechnung — links alone won't carry it.
7. **Don't over-trust attribution.** "Marketing is not math" — pick channels on fit +
   our strengths + feedback speed (our existing filter), not on a clean ROI number that
   social media won't give us anyway.
8. **Caution on the calculator thesis.** ChatGPT increasingly makes generic calculators
   pointless. Our calc bet only holds where the tool is *specific, embedded, and branded*
   — not "yet another generic Rechner" a user could just ask ChatGPT for.
